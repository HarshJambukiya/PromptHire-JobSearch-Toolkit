GuideFresher survival guide — what actually works
🎯The 30-day action plan for freshers
▼
Week 1 — Set up: Optimise LinkedIn (add skills matching job descriptions, professional photo, 70-word summary). Set Naukri job alerts. Complete 2–3 skill courses on Coursera/NPTEL.

Week 2 — Apply smart: Apply to 10–15 targeted jobs daily across 3–4 platforms. Apply within 24 hours of posting (3× better shortlist rate). Tailor your resume for each role — don't spam one generic resume.

Week 3 — Outreach: Send 10–15 cold emails or LinkedIn DMs to hiring managers. Target companies that got Series A/B funding in past 3–6 months (check YourStory, Inc42). Mention their recent product/news in your message.

Week 4 — Follow-up & build: Follow up on non-replies. Post one LinkedIn article about something you've built. Push your best project to GitHub with a proper README. This alone gets recruiter responses.
🚫Mistakes freshers make (and how to avoid them)
▼
Mistake 1: Mass applying without tailoring. Sending the same resume to 100 jobs gets you 0 calls. Tailor skills section to each job description's keywords.

Mistake 2: Only using one platform. Research shows candidates using 5+ platforms get 3× more recruiter views. Don't rely solely on LinkedIn or Naukri.

Mistake 3: Ignoring the hidden market. 70% of jobs are never posted. Cold emailing directly to a founder or team lead often creates an opening that didn't exist before.

Mistake 4: No GitHub / portfolio. In 2026, 80% of employers prioritise skills over degrees. A live GitHub with documented projects replaces work experience at the fresher level.

Mistake 5: No follow-up. 80% of candidates never follow up. A polite follow-up email after 5–7 days doubles your response rate.
🤖How to use AI tools for job searching (step by step)
▼
1. Generate your prompt using this tool above. Be specific about your skills and what makes you unique.

2. Open Claude.ai (free tier works) and enable Web Search in the tools panel. Paste the prompt. Claude searches live job boards and returns a structured table.

3. Use Perplexity.ai for real-time job searches — it searches the web by default. Paste the Job Search prompt there for even more results.

4. Use Cold Email / LinkedIn DM prompts in ChatGPT or Claude to write personalised outreach for specific companies. Always personalise by mentioning something specific about the company.

5. Use Interview Prep prompt before every interview. Tell Claude: "Act as a mock interviewer for [company]. Ask me technical + HR questions." Practice 2–3 full mock sessions.
💡What to do when you have no experience (for complete freshers)
▼
Build in public. A GitHub portfolio, Substack article, or LinkedIn case study series can replace work experience in 2026. Recruiters respond to people who show, not just tell.

Target micro-internships. Platforms like Internshala, Cuvette, and Unstop offer short 1–3 month paid internships recognised by 73% of Indian hiring managers as equivalent to traditional internships.

Participate in hackathons. Winning or placing in a challenge on Unstop, Kaggle, or HackWithInfy fast-tracks you past resume screening entirely.

Contribute to open source. Even 2–3 small GitHub contributions to known libraries (LangChain, Scikit-learn, Hugging Face) makes your profile stand out dramatically.

For AI/ML specifically: A working RAG pipeline demo, a live Streamlit app, or a documented Kaggle notebook is worth more than a generic resume at the fresher level.
